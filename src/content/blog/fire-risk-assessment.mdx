A Fire Risk Assessment (FRA) is a structured evaluation of potential fire hazards and safety measures within a property. For Phone Mast landlords, this assessment is crucial in ensuring that telecom infrastructure does not pose a fire risk to the site or surrounding areas. An FRA identifies fire hazards, assesses the level of risk, and recommends measures to mitigate those risks.

## Why is an FRA Required?

Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, landlords and those responsible for non-domestic properties must conduct a fire risk assessment to ensure safety and compliance. Additionally, with updates under the Building Safety Act 2022, there is an increasing focus on fire safety, requiring landlords to regularly review and update their FRA.

### For Phone Mast landlords, the key reasons for conducting an FRA include:

**Legal Compliance** – To meet fire safety legislation and avoid potential penalties.

**Risk Management** – Identifying fire hazards associated with telecom equipment, such as electrical faults or overheating.

**Liability Reduction** – Ensuring fire safety standards are met can protect landlords from liability in case of an incident.

**Tenant and Public Safety** – If the mast is on a shared property, it ensures the safety of tenants and the general public.

## When and Why Would an FRA Be Requested?

Telecom operators may request a Fire Risk Assessment for a number of reasons, including:

**Lease Agreements & Renewals** – As part of compliance checks when entering a new lease or renewing an existing agreement.

**Regulatory Compliance** – In response to updated fire safety laws or regulatory changes, such as those introduced in the Building Safety Act 2022.

**Site Inspections & Risk Assessments** – If an operator conducts a routine inspection and identifies potential fire risks, they may request an updated FRA.

**Installation of New Equipment** – When significant modifications or upgrades are made to a telecom site, operators may require confirmation that fire safety measures remain adequate.

**Insurance Requirements** – In some cases, insurers may request a fire risk assessment as part of their risk evaluation for the site.

Fire safety has always been a key consideration, but recent regulatory changes have brought increased scrutiny to compliance matters, including fire risk. The introduction of the Building Safety Act 2022 has reinforced the importance of regularly updated FRAs. While FRAs may not have been consistently requested in the past, telecom operators are now placing greater emphasis on ensuring their sites meet fire safety standards. As a result, landlords should expect requests for FRAs to become more frequent and standard practice moving forward.

### A Fire Risk Assessment typically involves:

**Identifying Fire Hazards** – Examining potential sources of ignition (e.g., electrical components) and fuel (e.g., cabling, enclosures, or vegetation near the site).

**Evaluating Risk to People** – Considering who could be affected, including maintenance workers, tenants, or nearby residents.

**Existing Fire Safety Measures** – Assessing the effectiveness of fire suppression systems, alarms, and emergency access routes.

**Recommendations for Improvement** – Outlining necessary actions such as fireproofing cables, installing extinguishers, or improving access for emergency services.

It is recommended that an FRA be reviewed at least annually and updated when:

- Significant modifications are made to the telecom site.

- New equipment is installed that could alter the fire risk.

- Changes in legislation or fire safety regulations occur.

## Who Conducts an FRA?

A Fire Risk Assessment should be carried out by a competent person—someone with the necessary expertise in fire safety regulations and risk assessments. This could be:

- A qualified fire safety consultant

- A specialist fire risk assessor

- A health and safety officer with fire safety training

## Final Thoughts

For Phone Mast landlords, fire safety is not just about compliance — it’s about protecting assets, tenants, and the surrounding environment. Regular FRAs help ensure that telecom installations remain safe and meet regulatory standards.
