## **Why Phone Mast Agreements Need Specialist Attention**

Phone Mast leases and Code agreements contain numerous technical clauses that can dramatically affect a landlord’s income and rights. Most landlords, and even many legal teams, aren’t aware of all the triggers, protections, opportunities, and pitfalls within these documents.

### **Some of the most common issues we identify include**:

- Rent levels that have not been reviewed when they should have been

- Operator “upgrade” requests that appear routine but can significantly reduce landlord rights

- Underpayments or missed rent hidden within operator administration or historic accounting

- Access requests that exceed what the agreement actually permits

- Damage to buildings, roofs, or access routes caused by mast installation or maintenance, with no repairs undertaken

- Incorrect or under-recovered electricity charges linked to mast equipment

- Inadequate or non-compliant risk assessments and method statements, raising health and safety concerns

- Lack of clarity around whether operators, contractors, and subcontractors are properly briefed on site-specific health and safety risks

- Equipment upgrades that risk overloading roofs or structures beyond their original design limits

- Opportunities within older or pre-Code leases that are frequently overlooked or misunderstood

Without specialist knowledge, these issues can remain hidden for years
